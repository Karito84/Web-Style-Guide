Text Styles

Headers:

<h1>

use it only for the title of the page

  • font-weight: 700
  • font-size: 32px
  • font-family: Lato

<h2>

use it for headings - major sections of the page to draw user's attention

  • font-weight: 700
  • font-size: 24px
  • font-family: Lato

<h3>...<h6>

use them for subheadings - smaller focal points, such as articles, to make user notice them, when scanning the page

  • font-weight: 500
  • font-size: 21px
  • font-family: Frank Ruhl Libre

Paragraphs:

<p>

  • font-weight: 400
  • font-size: 16px
  • font-family: Open Sans

Flat Buttons Facts

  • simpler in design
  • rectangles, rounded rectangles or circles
  • visually distinct from other elements
  • have specific, descriptive labels

Important! Buttons should make use of :hover and :active states and the cursor: pointer declaration.

Skeuomorphic Buttons Facts

  • imitate a real- life button
  • ‘raised’ appearance while unpressed, a ‘pressed’ appearance when clicked
  • CSS styles are preferred over images
  • use of hover and/or active states is important
